# Final 9-Domain Structure
## Comprehensive Analysis-Driven Design

---

## Design Summary

Based on comprehensive analysis of all 1,570 anchor points, this structure:
- Re-sorts content by **thematic lean** (distinctive character)
- Follows **prerequisite sequences** for optimal scaffolding
- Implements **Big 4 dispersed model** (positions 1, 2, 5, 8)
- Applies **fundamental/mastery percentages** per specifications

## Domain 1: Psychometrics & Scientific Foundations

### Character
**Thematic Lean:** Statistical reasoning & validity
**Personality:** "The methodologist" - rigorous, evidence-driven, foundational

### Why This Lean?
Statistical reasoning underlies ALL psychological knowledge claims. This domain answers: "How do we KNOW what we know?" Every subsequent domain builds on the ability to evaluate evidence.

### Content Mapping

| Source | Subdomain | Lean Justification |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| RMS | Types of Variables and Data | Foundational statistical concepts |
| RMS | Research - Internal/External Validity | Evaluating evidence quality |
| RMS | Research - Single-Subject and Group Designs | Understanding how knowledge is generated |
| RMS | Overview of Inferential Statistics | Statistical reasoning foundations |
| RMS | Inferential Statistical Tests | Hypothesis testing logic |
| RMS | Correlation and Regression | Prediction and relationship analysis |
| TES | Item Analysis and Test Reliability | Measurement quality (reliability focus) |
| TES | Test Validity - Content and Construct | Measurement quality (validity focus) |
| TES | Test Validity - Criterion-Related | Predictive accuracy |
| LEA | Classical Conditioning (theory only) | Foundational learning mechanism |
| LEA | Operant Conditioning (theory only) | Foundational learning mechanism |

### Fundamental vs Mastery Split (60/40)
- **Fundamental (60%):** Basic statistics, research design concepts, reliability/validity definitions, conditioning principles
- **Mastery (40%):** Applying statistics to evaluate research, choosing appropriate designs, critiquing studies

### Prerequisite Role
This domain provides the foundation for:
- Understanding treatment outcome research (Domain 4)
- Interpreting assessment validity (Domain 8)
- Evaluating evidence-based practice (all domains)

### Estimated Questions: ~200

## Domain 2: Developmental Psychology

### Character
**Thematic Lean:** Stage theories & critical periods
**Personality:** "The developmentalist" - lifespan-focused, milestone-oriented, sequential

### Why This Lean?
Development unfolds in predictable stages with critical/sensitive periods. Understanding NORMAL development is prerequisite to identifying ABNORMAL development (psychopathology).

### Content Mapping

| Source | Subdomain | Lean Justification |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| LIF | Cognitive Development | Piaget's stages, sequential progression |
| LIF | Physical Development | Motor milestones, puberty timing |
| LIF | Language Development | Sequential acquisition, critical periods |
| LIF | Socioemotional - Attachment | Bowlby's phases, Ainsworth patterns |
| LIF | Socioemotional - Temperament | Early appearing, biologically based traits |
| LIF | Socioemotional - Moral Development | Kohlberg's stages |
| LIF | Early Influences - Nature vs Nurture | Critical periods, sensitive periods |
| LIF | Early Influences - Prenatal | Teratogens, timing effects |
| LIF | School and Family Influences | Contextual effects on development |

### Fundamental vs Mastery Split (55/45)
- **Fundamental (55%):** Stage sequences, milestone timing, attachment patterns, temperament types
- **Mastery (45%):** Applying developmental knowledge to cases, recognizing atypical patterns, predicting outcomes

### Prerequisite Role
This domain provides the foundation for:
- Recognizing developmental disorders (Domain 3)
- Understanding treatment adaptations for age (Domain 4)
- Assessing children/adolescents (Domain 8)

### Estimated Questions: ~175

## Domain 3: Clinical Psychopathology

### Character
**Thematic Lean:** Genetics & etiology
**Personality:** "The nosologist" - classification-focused, etiology-driven, diagnostic

### Why This Lean?
Psychopathology emphasizes CAUSES - genetic vulnerabilities, biological mechanisms, diathesis-stress models. The focus is on WHY disorders develop, not just WHAT the symptoms are.

### Content Mapping

| Source | Subdomain | Lean Justification |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| PPA | Neurodevelopmental Disorders | Genetic basis, early emergence |
| PPA | Schizophrenia Spectrum | Dopamine hypothesis, heritability |
| PPA | Bipolar and Depressive Disorders | Genetic loading, neurochemistry |
| PPA | Anxiety Disorders and OCD | Biological vulnerability, conditioning |
| PPA | Trauma/Stressor-Related | Stress-diathesis, biological response |
| PPA | Feeding/Eating Disorders | Biological and psychological etiology |
| PPA | Disruptive/Impulse-Control | Neurological and temperament factors |
| PPA | Substance-Related Disorders | Genetics of addiction, neuroadaptation |
| PPA | Neurocognitive Disorders | Neuropathology, genetic risk factors |
| PPA | Personality Disorders | Temperament + environment interaction |
| PPA | Sexual Dysfunctions/Paraphilias | Biological and learned components |

### Fundamental vs Mastery Split (30/70)
- **Fundamental (30%):** DSM criteria, symptom recognition, basic etiology
- **Mastery (70%):** Differential diagnosis, comorbidity analysis, etiological formulation, case conceptualization

### Prerequisite Role
Requires: Domain 2 (normal development)
Enables: Domain 4 (treatment selection)

### Estimated Questions: ~210

## Domain 4: Therapeutic Psychology

### Character
**Thematic Lean:** Mechanisms of change
**Personality:** "The interventionist" - process-focused, technique-oriented, change-driven

### Why This Lean?
The emphasis is on HOW therapy produces change - not just what techniques exist, but WHY they work. Understanding mechanisms enables flexible, adaptive practice.

### Content Mapping

| Source | Subdomain | Lean Justification |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| CLI | Cognitive-Behavioral Therapies | Cognitive restructuring mechanism |
| CLI | Psychodynamic/Humanistic | Insight and awareness mechanisms |
| CLI | Family and Group Therapies | Systemic change mechanisms |
| CLI | Brief Therapies | Rapid change mechanisms |
| CLI | Prevention and Consultation | Preventive mechanisms, consultation process |
| LEA | Interventions - Classical Conditioning | Extinction, counterconditioning mechanisms |
| LEA | Interventions - Operant Conditioning | Reinforcement-based change mechanisms |
| LEA | Memory and Forgetting | Memory modification mechanisms |

### Fundamental vs Mastery Split (25/75)
- **Fundamental (25%):** Basic therapy models, technique definitions
- **Mastery (75%):** Selecting mechanisms for specific cases, adapting techniques, evaluating outcomes, integrating modalities

### Prerequisite Role
Requires: Domain 1 (outcome research), Domain 2 (developmental adaptations), Domain 3 (diagnosis)
Enables: Evidence-based treatment planning

### Estimated Questions: ~170

## Domain 5: Social & Cultural Psychology

### Character
**Thematic Lean:** Identity & group dynamics
**Personality:** "The contextualist" - identity-focused, culturally-attuned, systems-aware

### Why This Lean?
Identity (individual and cultural) shapes how people perceive, behave, and respond to intervention. Group dynamics explain social influence. This domain answers: "Who is this person in their social world?"

### Content Mapping

| Source | Subdomain | Lean Justification |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| SOC | Social Cognition - Attributions | How identity shapes perception |
| SOC | Social Cognition - Biases/Heuristics | Identity-based cognitive patterns |
| SOC | Attitudes and Attitude Change | Identity-consistent attitudes |
| SOC | Persuasion | Influence on identity and beliefs |
| SOC | Social Influence - Group | Group identity, conformity |
| SOC | Social Influence - Types | Normative and informational influence |
| SOC | Affiliation and Attraction | Interpersonal identity |
| SOC | Prosocial/Prejudice | Ingroup/outgroup dynamics |
| CLI | Cross-Cultural - Terms/Concepts | Cultural identity constructs |
| CLI | Cross-Cultural - Identity Models | Racial/cultural identity development |

### Fundamental vs Mastery Split (50/50)
- **Fundamental (50%):** Attribution theory, bias types, group processes, cultural identity stages
- **Mastery (50%):** Applying cultural competence, analyzing group dynamics, adapting interventions culturally

### Prerequisite Role
Big 4 reinforcement mid-sequence. Contextualizes individual psychology within social systems.

### Estimated Questions: ~155

## Domain 7: Biopsychology

### Character
**Thematic Lean:** Brain anatomy & neural circuits
**Personality:** "The neuroscientist" - structure-focused, localization-oriented, circuit-mapping

### Why This Lean?
Understanding WHERE in the brain processes occur and HOW neural circuits function. Emphasis on structure-function relationships, lesion effects, and neural communication.

### Content Mapping

| Source | Subdomain | Lean Justification |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| PHY | Brain Regions - Hindbrain/Midbrain/Subcortical | Anatomical structures and functions |
| PHY | Brain Regions - Cerebral Cortex | Cortical localization |
| PHY | Nervous System, Neurons, Neurotransmitters | Neural circuit communication |
| PHY | Sensation and Perception | Sensory circuit processing |
| PHY | Memory and Sleep | Memory circuit consolidation |
| PHY | Emotions and Stress | Limbic circuit function |
| PHY | Neurological and Endocrine Disorders | Circuit dysfunction |

### Fundamental vs Mastery Split (25/75)
- **Fundamental (25%):** Brain structure names, neurotransmitter types, basic circuit concepts
- **Mastery (75%):** Predicting lesion effects, interpreting neuroimaging, understanding circuit dysfunction in disorders

### Prerequisite Role
Requires: Domain 1 (research foundations)
Enables: Domain 9 (pharmacology - how drugs affect circuits)

**Note:** Neurotransmitters are introduced here (delayed as requested)

### Estimated Questions: ~145

## Domain 8: Psychological Assessment

### Character
**Thematic Lean:** Clinical interpretation & profiles
**Personality:** "The diagnostician" - interpretation-focused, profile-oriented, integrative

### Why This Lean?
The emphasis is on INTERPRETING test results - recognizing patterns, understanding profiles, making clinical inferences. Not just knowing tests exist, but knowing what scores MEAN.

### Content Mapping

| Source | Subdomain | Lean Justification |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| PAS | Stanford-Binet and Wechsler Tests | IQ profile interpretation |
| PAS | Other Cognitive Ability Measures | Cognitive pattern analysis |
| PAS | MMPI-2 | Validity and clinical scale profiles |
| PAS | Other Personality Measures | Personality profile interpretation |
| PAS | Clinical Tests | Screening interpretation |
| PAS | Interest Inventories | Career profile interpretation |
| TES | Test Score Interpretation | Converting and comparing scores |

### Fundamental vs Mastery Split (45/55)
- **Fundamental (45%):** Score types, test purposes, basic interpretation rules
- **Mastery (55%):** Profile analysis, pattern recognition, integrated interpretation, clinical decision-making

### Prerequisite Role
Big 4 reinforcement late-sequence. Integrates psychometric foundations (Domain 1) with clinical application.

### Estimated Questions: ~85

## Domain 9: Psychopharmacology & Professional Ethics

### Character
**Thematic Lean:** Drug mechanisms & ethical decision-making
**Personality:** "The integrator" - mechanism-focused, principle-driven, professionally-grounded

### Why This Lean?
Two complementary foci: (1) HOW drugs work at the neurochemical level, and (2) HOW to navigate ethical complexity. Both require sophisticated decision-making and integration of prior knowledge.

### Content Mapping

| Source | Subdomain | Lean Justification |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| PHY | Psychopharmacology - Antipsychotics/Antidepressants | Drug mechanisms, receptor effects |
| PHY | Psychopharmacology - Other Drugs | Anxiolytics, mood stabilizers, mechanisms |
| ETH | Ethics Code Overview, Standards 1 & 2 | Foundational ethical principles |
| ETH | Standards 3 & 4 | Relationships and confidentiality decisions |
| ETH | Standards 5 & 6 | Advertising and record-keeping decisions |
| ETH | Standards 7 & 8 | Education and research ethics |
| ETH | Standards 9 & 10 | Assessment and therapy ethics |
| ETH | Professional Issues | Legal and professional decision-making |

### Fundamental vs Mastery Split (20/80)
- **Fundamental (20%):** Drug classes, basic ethics code structure
- **Mastery (80%):** Predicting drug effects/interactions, navigating ethical dilemmas, integrating biological and ethical considerations

### Prerequisite Role
CAPSTONE domain. Requires integration of:
- Domain 7 (neural circuits for drug mechanisms)
- All clinical domains (for ethical scenarios)
- Research foundations (for research ethics)

**Note:** Pharmacology positioned last as requested

### Estimated Questions: ~230
