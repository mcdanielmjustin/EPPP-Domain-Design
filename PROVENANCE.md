# EPPP Content Provenance

This document establishes the independent-creation chain for the PassEPPP
content system: where our chapter/domain structure, anchor taxonomy, and
question text come from, and how we have documented each step.

**Purpose**: evidence of independent creation per 17 U.S.C. § 106 and
*Feist v. Rural Telephone Service Co.* (1991) — facts are not copyrightable;
creative expression is. Our content is built from public-domain factual
sources, with all expression independently authored or paraphrased.

---

## 1. Source hierarchy — where our content derives from

1. **EPPP Candidate Handbook** (ASPPB — Association of State and Provincial
   Psychology Boards). The official, publicly published exam-content outline
   used by licensing boards across US/Canada. This is our PRIMARY source for
   what to teach and in what depth.
   - Used for: domain selection, knowledge-area (KN1–KN71) alignment, exam
     percentage weighting.
   - Copyright status: published by a non-profit licensing body as a public
     study guide; the fact-list it contains (e.g. "DSM-5-TR diagnostic
     criteria," "major psychotherapy orientations") consists of
     non-copyrightable factual outlines.

2. **Named psychology textbooks and primary-source journal articles**.
   Examples: Linehan (1993) for DBT; Beck (1976) for cognitive therapy;
   Kohlberg (1983) for moral development; Pavlov (1927) for classical
   conditioning; APA (2017) Ethics Code; DSM-5-TR (APA 2022).
   - Used for: factual anchor content, primary-source citation format in
     anchor_content_summary values.
   - Copyright status: quotation and paraphrase of factual content from
     named scholarly sources is fair use for educational commentary; our
     expression is rewritten.

3. **Independently authored question text and explanations**.
   Our question stems, distractor options, correct-answer rationales, and
   multi-option explanations were generated by our own AI-assisted authoring
   pipeline using our chapter organization, our paraphrased anchor summaries,
   and our misconception taxonomy as inputs. No PrepJet source text was
   fed into the pipeline as reference.

## 3. Structural-organization independence

Our 9-domain structure (PMET, LDEV, CPAT, PTHE, SOCU, WDEV, BPSY, CASS,
PETH) covers the same knowledge territory as the EPPP Candidate Handbook's
8 content areas, reorganized into 9 for pedagogical granularity. Chapter
titles and ordering do not match any competitor's outline (109 competitor
chapter titles vs our 121 — zero title overlap as of the 2026-04-19 audit).

### Identifier system (independent)

Our canonical `uid` format is `D{n}-{SUB}-{bare}-{hex8}` — e.g.
`D1-LEA-009-f89cf513`. The hex suffix is a deterministic hash of
(domain, subdomain, bare_id, verbatim_text), making every UID globally
unique and content-addressed. This is a proprietary identifier scheme
designed independently; the only pre-existing shared element is the
three-letter subdomain-code namespace (PHY, PPA, LIF, etc.), which uses
standard psychology abbreviations defensible as independent choice of
natural shorthand.

### Competitive moat (unrelated to copyright but load-bearing for product)

Bloom's-taxonomy tagging on every question (`blooms_primary`,
`blooms_secondary`), misconception-type metadata on every distractor
(`misconception_id`, `misconception_label`, `misconception_type`,
`distractor_level`), and our adaptive-flashcard spawn logic built atop
these tags are a differentiator not matched by any competitor. These
columns are HARD-LOCKED against any copyright-remediation rewrite — they
are our own independent work product.

  pre-rewrite text alongside post-rewrite text for every anchor.

## 5. Ongoing protections

The following mechanisms guard against future regressions toward
third-party phrasing:

- **`verify_distractors_unchanged.py`** — CI-runnable check that the
  HARD-LOCKED columns (option_a/b/c/d, correct_answer, explanation,
  tested_concept, misconception_*, distractor_level, blooms_*) match their
  pre-remediation baseline byte-for-byte.
- **`copyright_sentinel.py`** (forthcoming, Tier E1) — nightly 5-gram
  overlap scan of shipped artifacts against the third-party reference
  corpus. Alerts on any row exceeding a 40% overlap threshold.
- **Pre-commit hook** (forthcoming, Tier E2) — blocks any commit
  introducing `PrepJet`, `EPPP-P{1..7}-`, `EPPP-F{1..9}-`, or `-PPY-`
  substrings under `content/` paths.
- **`STYLE_GUIDE.md`** — mandatory reading for all content-author
  contributors; requires primary-source citation for paraphrased facts,
  prohibits direct copy-paste from the third-party source CSV, and
  requires the copyright sentinel to pass before merge.
